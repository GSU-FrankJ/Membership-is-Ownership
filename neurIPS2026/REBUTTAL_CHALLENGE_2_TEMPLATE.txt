================================================================================
REBUTTAL TEMPLATE: Challenge 2 — Black-Box LoRA as Current Standard Threat Model
================================================================================

CHALLENGE (expected):
"WDM/Zhao also show robustness. Why is your γ-recipe threat model new or
justified compared to prior work?"

REBUTTAL RESPONSE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We clarify the evolution of threat model standards in diffusion watermarking
literature.

(a) WDM (Zhao et al., 2023, arXiv 2303.10137) did not evaluate fine-tuning
    robustness.

    WDM's original evaluation scope includes:
    ✓ Clean-generation watermark recovery (bit-string extraction on EDM;
      trigger-based image-text pair reconstruction on SD)
    ✓ Distortion robustness (blur, noise, JPEG, etc.)
    ✗ LoRA fine-tuning robustness — not evaluated
    ✗ Full-parameter fine-tuning robustness — not evaluated
    ✗ White-box adaptive attacks — not in scope

    This is not a limitation of WDM per se—it reflects the publication
    timeline. WDM (Mar 2023) predates the widespread adoption of
    parameter-efficient fine-tuning in production diffusion pipelines.

(b) Fine-tuning robustness is the new evaluation standard, established
    by concurrent and follow-up work.

    SleeperMark (Wang et al., 2024, arXiv 2412.04852) explicitly frames
    fine-tuning robustness as a critical evaluation dimension missing
    from prior work. SleeperMark demonstrates watermark survival under
    LoRA adaptation (rank 20–640) across multiple domains, motivating
    fine-tuning as the realistic threat model for modern SD deployments.

(c) Our γ-recipe threat model aligns with the emerging SD watermarking
    literature standard.

    γ-recipe = black-box, domain-adaptive fine-tuning with pure MSE loss
    (no decoder awareness, no memorization-specific optimization).
    This matches SleeperMark's evaluation protocol (LoRA rank 20–640,
    DreamBooth / Naruto datasets, ControlNet adaptation). By adopting
    this threat model, we measure robustness in the setting that the
    most recent SD watermarking work has identified as critical.

(d) White-box adaptive attacks are explicitly out of scope.

    A stronger threat—white-box fine-tuning with decoder-aware loss
    (explicitly minimizing reconstruction error on W or maximizing
    memorization erasure)—falls outside the black-box domain-adaptation
    setting. Such adaptive attacks require adversary knowledge of watermark
    internals and are deferred to future work.

SUMMARY:
Our γ-recipe is not an arbitrary threat model; it is the standard that
SleeperMark (the most recent watermarking work on SD) has established. By
evaluating against this standard, we demonstrate that robustness to realistic
fine-tuning is achievable without training overhead or FID penalties.
